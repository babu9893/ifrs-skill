# IFRS Compliance Templates

Use these templates when producing structured compliance deliverables. Each template includes header fields, a tabular body, and summary sections. Adapt column widths and detail to the engagement scope.

## 2. Gap Analysis Template

### Purpose

Identify and assess differences between an entity's current accounting treatment and the requirements of an IFRS standard. Used during first-time adoption, standard transitions, or compliance health-checks.

### Header Fields

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Entity** | [Legal entity name] |
| **Assessment Date** | [DD/MM/YYYY] |
| **Scope** | [e.g., Transition to IFRS 16 / Full IFRS adoption] |
| **Current Framework** | [e.g., Local GAAP, US GAAP, IFRS (prior version)] |
| **Prepared by** | [Name, title] |
| **Reviewed by** | [Name, title] |

### Impact Scale Definitions

| Rating | Definition |
|---|---|
| **High (H)** | Material quantitative impact on financial statements (>5% of relevant line item or >1% of total assets/revenue); significant change to accounting policy; potential restatement or modified audit opinion if unaddressed |
| **Medium (M)** | Moderate quantitative impact (1-5% of relevant line item); requires policy change or new processes but unlikely to affect audit opinion on its own |
| **Low (L)** | Immaterial quantitative impact (<1% of relevant line item); primarily a disclosure enhancement or presentational change; limited systems or process change required |

## 3. Audit Memo Format

### Purpose

Document the analysis of a specific accounting issue with sufficient rigour to support the audit file. The memo should stand alone -- a reviewer unfamiliar with the engagement should be able to follow the logic from facts through to conclusion.

### Memo Structure

```
TECHNICAL ACCOUNTING MEMORANDUM

Entity:          [Legal entity name]
Prepared by:     [Name, title]
Date:            [DD/MM/YYYY]
Reviewed by:     [Name, title]
Review Date:     [DD/MM/YYYY]
Reference:       [Engagement / workpaper reference]

---------------------------------------------------------------

1. ISSUE
   [Concise statement of the accounting question to be resolved.
    One to three sentences.]

2. APPLICABLE STANDARDS
   - [IFRS X -- Title (paragraph references)]
   - [IFRS Y -- Title (paragraph references)]
   - [Other relevant guidance: IFRIC, agenda decisions, etc.]

3. FACTS AND CIRCUMSTANCES
   [Narrative description of the transaction(s), contract terms,
    business context, and any other relevant facts. Include
    amounts where available.]

4. ANALYSIS

   4.1 [Sub-issue 1 title]
       [Analysis with explicit reference to standard paragraphs.
        Apply the requirements to the specific facts. Address
        alternatives considered and why they were accepted or
        rejected.]

   4.2 [Sub-issue 2 title]
       [Continue for each sub-issue.]

   4.3 [Sub-issue n title]
       [...]

5. CONCLUSION
   [Compliant / Non-compliant / Judgment required]
   [Summary of conclusion with clear link back to analysis.
    State the accounting treatment to be applied.]

6. JOURNAL ENTRY IMPACT
   [Specific journal entries with amounts, accounts, and timing.
    Show Dr/Cr clearly.]

7. DISCLOSURE IMPACT
   [Identify which notes in the financial statements are affected
    and what disclosures are required or need updating.]

8. RECOMMENDATION
   [Action items for the engagement team or the client, including
    any further procedures required.]
```

### Worked Example: Revenue Recognition for Multi-Element Software Arrangement (IFRS 15)

```
TECHNICAL ACCOUNTING MEMORANDUM

Entity:          NovaTech Solutions Inc.
Prepared by:     A. Morales, Senior Manager
Date:            05/03/2026
Reviewed by:     K. Lindgren, Partner
Review Date:     12/03/2026
Reference:       NT-2025-REV-003

---------------------------------------------------------------

1. ISSUE

NovaTech entered into a CU 2,400,000 contract with Meridian
Financial Services on 1 July 2025 for (a) a perpetual software
license, (b) implementation and customisation services, and
(c) three years of post-contract support (PCS) including
unspecified software updates and telephone/online support.

The question is how to identify the performance obligations,
allocate the transaction price, and determine the timing of
revenue recognition under IFRS 15.

2. APPLICABLE STANDARDS

- IFRS 15 Revenue from Contracts with Customers
  (paragraphs 22-30: identifying performance obligations;
   paragraphs 47-72: allocating the transaction price;
   paragraphs 31-45: determining the transaction price;
   paragraphs 35-38: variable consideration;
   paragraphs 73-90: satisfying performance obligations)
- IFRS 15.B34-B41: Licenses of intellectual property
- IFRS 15.IE -- Illustrative Examples (Examples 54-62:
  licensing, combined goods and services)

3. FACTS AND CIRCUMSTANCES

Contract details:

- Customer: Meridian Financial Services (large regional bank)
- Contract date: 1 July 2025
- Total stated price: CU 2,400,000
  - Perpetual license fee: CU 1,200,000 (payable on delivery)
  - Implementation/customisation: CU 600,000 (payable in three
    milestones over 6 months)
  - PCS: CU 200,000 per annum for 3 years (CU 600,000 total,
    payable annually in advance)
- The perpetual license grants Meridian the right to use
  NovaTech's core banking analytics platform (version 8.2).
- Implementation involves configuration, data migration, and
  customisation of standard reports. Estimated duration is
  6 months (July 2025 -- December 2025). The customisation
  modifies existing report templates but does not alter the
  underlying software code.
- PCS includes (i) unspecified software updates ("when-and-if
  available") and (ii) telephone and online technical support
  during business hours.
- NovaTech regularly sells the perpetual license on a standalone
  basis for CU 1,000,000 to CU 1,300,000 (median CU 1,150,000).
- NovaTech sells implementation services for comparable-scope
  projects at CU 500,000 to CU 700,000 (median CU 600,000).
- NovaTech sells PCS renewals at CU 180,000 to CU 220,000 per
  annum (median CU 200,000 per annum; CU 600,000 for 3 years).
- No significant financing component (all payments within
  12 months of the related performance).
- No variable consideration, rights of return, or penalties.
- The software is functional without the implementation services
  (a standard installation is available), but Meridian requires
  the customisation for operational reasons.

4. ANALYSIS

4.1 Identification of Performance Obligations (IFRS 15.22-30)

A promised good or service is a separate performance obligation
if it is (a) capable of being distinct (IFRS 15.27(a)) and
(b) distinct within the context of the contract (IFRS 15.27(b)).

(a) Perpetual license:
    The software is functional on delivery using a standard
    installation. Meridian could benefit from the license on its
    own or with readily available resources. The license is
    capable of being distinct.

    The implementation services do not significantly modify or
    customise the underlying source code; they configure and
    adapt standard report templates. NovaTech and other vendors
    offer implementation services independently. The license is
    not highly interrelated with or dependent on the
    implementation services within the context of this contract.

    Conclusion: The perpetual license is a separate performance
    obligation.

(b) Implementation and customisation services:
    These services are available from NovaTech and third-party
    consultants independently of the license (several certified
    partners offer equivalent services). They are capable of
    being distinct.

    The implementation does not significantly transform or
    integrate with the license into a combined output -- the
    deliverable is a configured software environment, not a
    fundamentally different product. The services are distinct
    within the context of the contract.

    Conclusion: Implementation/customisation is a separate
    performance obligation.

(c) Post-contract support (PCS):
    PCS (unspecified updates plus technical support) is routinely
    sold separately as a renewal. It is capable of being distinct
    and is separately identifiable in the contract.

    Conclusion: PCS is a separate performance obligation.

    Note: The unspecified updates and the technical support within
    PCS are not separately accounted for because they form a
    single series of services with the same pattern of transfer
    (stand-ready obligation satisfied over time). They are treated
    as one performance obligation.

Summary: Three performance obligations identified.

4.2 Determining the Transaction Price (IFRS 15.47-72)

The total transaction price is CU 2,400,000. There is no
variable consideration, significant financing component, or
non-cash consideration. The transaction price is fixed.

4.3 Allocating the Transaction Price (IFRS 15.73-90)

IFRS 15.76-80 requires allocation based on relative standalone
selling prices (SSP). Observable SSPs exist for all three
performance obligations:

| Performance Obligation | SSP (median) | Relative % | Allocated Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| Perpetual license | CU 1,150,000 | 48.94% | CU 1,174,468 |
| Implementation | CU 600,000 | 25.53% | CU 612,766 |
| PCS (3 years) | CU 600,000 | 25.53% | CU 612,766 |
| **Total** | **CU 2,350,000** | **100.00%** | **CU 2,400,000** |

Calculation: Allocated price = (SSP / Total SSP) x CU 2,400,000

The CU 50,000 premium over the sum of SSPs is distributed
proportionally across all three obligations. No residual
approach or adjusted market assessment is necessary because
observable standalone prices are available.

4.4 Timing of Revenue Recognition (IFRS 15.31-45, B34-B41)

(a) Perpetual license -- point in time or over time?

    Under IFRS 15.B56, a license of intellectual property is
    either a right to access (over time) or a right to use
    (point in time).

    A perpetual license to software version 8.2, where the
    customer is not required to use updated versions and the
    software has significant standalone functionality, represents
    a right to use intellectual property (IFRS 15.B58).

    NovaTech's ongoing activities (PCS updates) do not
    significantly affect the utility of version 8.2 to which
    the customer already has a right. The customer can use
    version 8.2 indefinitely without updates.

    Conclusion: Revenue for the perpetual license is recognised
    at a point in time -- when the customer obtains control,
    i.e., when the license key is delivered and the software is
    available for download/installation. This occurred on
    15 July 2025.

    Revenue recognised: CU 1,174,468 on 15 July 2025.

(b) Implementation services -- point in time or over time?

    IFRS 15.35(a): Meridian simultaneously receives and consumes
    the benefits as NovaTech performs (configuration work enhances
    Meridian's environment progressively). If NovaTech were
    replaced, a new provider would not need to substantially
    re-perform work completed to date.

    Alternatively, under IFRS 15.35(c): NovaTech's performance
    creates an asset (configured environment) that the customer
    controls as it is created.

    Either criterion is met. Implementation revenue is recognised
    over time.

    Measure of progress: input method based on labour hours
    incurred relative to total estimated hours (IFRS 15.41-43).
    This faithfully depicts NovaTech's performance because the
    services involve a relatively homogeneous effort profile.

    At 31 December 2025:
    - Total estimated hours: 2,400
    - Hours incurred to date: 2,160
    - Percentage complete: 90%
    - Revenue recognised to date: CU 612,766 x 90% = CU 551,489
    - Revenue recognised in H2 2025: CU 551,489
    - Remaining to recognise in Q1 2026: CU 61,277

(c) PCS -- point in time or over time?

    PCS is a stand-ready obligation. IFRS 15.35(a) is met:
    the customer simultaneously receives and consumes the
    benefits of NovaTech's stand-ready performance (availability
    of support and updates) as NovaTech performs.

    Revenue is recognised over time. Because the stand-ready
    service is provided uniformly over the support period,
    a time-based measure (straight-line) is appropriate.

    PCS period: 1 July 2025 to 30 June 2028 (3 years).

    Revenue per month: CU 612,766 / 36 months = CU 17,021.

    Revenue recognised in 2025 (6 months): CU 102,128
    Revenue to recognise in 2026 (12 months): CU 204,255
    Revenue to recognise in 2027 (12 months): CU 204,255
    Revenue to recognise in 2028 (6 months): CU 102,128

5. CONCLUSION

   Compliant -- the arrangement contains three distinct
   performance obligations. The proposed treatment is:

   - Perpetual license (CU 1,174,468): point in time on
     delivery (15 July 2025)
   - Implementation (CU 612,766): over time using input
     method (percentage of completion by labour hours)
   - PCS (CU 612,766): over time on a straight-line basis
     over 36 months

   This treatment is consistent with IFRS 15 and supported by
   observable standalone selling prices.

6. JOURNAL ENTRY IMPACT

   (a) 15 July 2025 -- License delivery and first PCS payment

       Dr  Trade receivables          CU 1,200,000
       Dr  Trade receivables          CU 200,000
           Cr  Revenue -- License                     CU 1,174,468
           Cr  Contract liability -- Implementation       CU 24,043
           Cr  Contract liability -- PCS                  CU 97,872
           Cr  Revenue -- PCS (Jul 2025)                 CU 17,021

               pre-paid portion: Aug-Jun)                CU 86,596

       Note: CU 200,000 PCS payment received in advance
       covers Jul 2025 - Jun 2026. Of the CU 1,200,000 license
       payment, CU 1,174,468 is license revenue and CU 25,532
```

## Usage Notes

- **Professional judgment**: Templates structure the analysis but do not replace judgment. All conclusions should be reviewed by a qualified professional.
